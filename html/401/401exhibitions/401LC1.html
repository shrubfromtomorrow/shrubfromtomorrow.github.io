Capstone
My capstone started with me being mildly irritated. I had to buy a graphing calculator for the ACT and found the selection limited.
Adding to my anger was the quality of the $150 product. The machine was slow, poorly made, seemed to have had no user testing, and I have really only used it thrice (programming on it doesn't count).
I want to solve this. I want to create an entire calculator. From user interface to hardware, I think I could improve almost everything that Texas Instruments has done or not done.
After some very illuminating research, I found that the root of the problem I wanted to tackle was deeper than I thought. TI has a solid monopoly, they can do whatever they want in terms of pricing. Teachers are only taught course material through TI calculators and most College Board exams only accept TI calculators (College Board is also a cesspool of a company).
If I was going to try to solve this, it would be tough to break through TI's layers of lobbying and general sketchy practices. I decided to set my sights on proof of concept rather than a full takeover.
The first thing I needed to work on was the calculator UI. I wanted to get a quick prototype ready with a Raspberry Pi and a linux kernel install. However, Raspberry Pis are impossible to get commercially and so I do not have an option for physical prototyping yet. I decided to focus my energy on developing the actual calculator interface as making progress physically was effectively stonewalled.
I (as you can see here) have had some experience with web development so I decided to write the calculator application in JavaScript as, when using the Electron library, I am able to develop a website that loads natively on any desktop.
